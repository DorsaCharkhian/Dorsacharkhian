Drexel One AI Chatbot

Inclusive AI-powered UX research for international students

UX Research Lead · Academic Research Project

Led AI-powered UX research improving accessibility and achieving 80% higher satisfaction and 89% task success for international student populations.

Context & Constraints

  • Grant-funded academic research project with live international student participants
  • Led a cross-functional research, design, and development team
  • Balanced research depth with limited development resources
  • Focused on users with varying language proficiency and accessibility needs

Problem

International students struggled with navigation, language clarity, and accessibility in DrexelOne, leading to task failure and frustration.

My Role & Responsibilities

  • Led UX research strategy
  • Secured research funding and coordinated with designers and developers
  • Designed multilingual and accessibility-focused studies
  • Synthesized qualitative and behavioral data
  • Collaborated with faculty and students

What I Did

  • Identified key barriers through interviews, surveys, and task testing
  • Designed AI-assisted support concepts to reduce cognitive load
  • Prioritized cross-cultural clarity over direct translation

Research scope: 30 user interviews and 50 A/B tests across multilingual task flows.

Key Research Insight

Early usability testing revealed that direct translation increased confusion rather than clarity for international students. Based on this insight, we shifted toward concept-based explanations and AI-assisted guidance to reduce cognitive load. This insight directly changed the design direction.

Impact

+80% user satisfaction
+89% task success rate
Improved comprehension for Farsi-, Spanish-, and Chinese-speaking users

Key Takeaway

This project shows how AI can be used to enhance inclusivity without replacing human judgment.

Why this matters: Teams building AI-driven products for diverse users need research that prioritizes clarity, trust, and decision-making—not just feature validation.
