Supporting Parents in Childcare

Trust-centered UX research and service design for healthcare

UX Researcher & Service Designer

Designed a healthcare support service to help parents make informed, low-stress decisions in non-emergency childcare situations through trust-centered UX research and service design.

Problem

Parents often face uncertainty and stress when making childcare decisions in non-emergency situations, lacking clear, trustworthy guidance to support informed choices.

My Role & Responsibilities

  • Led UX research and service design for a parent-facing healthcare support experience
  • Conducted user research to understand decision-making and trust needs
  • Designed service touchpoints to reduce stress and support informed decisions
  • Collaborated with stakeholders to align research with service delivery

What I Did

  • Identified key barriers to informed, low-stress decision-making through interviews and journey mapping
  • Co-designed service concepts with parents and providers
  • Prioritized trust and accessibility in information architecture and content

Impact

Trust-centered service design
Informed parent decision-making
Healthcare UX & accessibility focus

Key Takeaway

Trust-centered UX research and service design can help parents make informed, low-stress decisions in childcare by putting clarity and accessibility at the center of the experience.
