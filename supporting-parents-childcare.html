Supporting Parents in Childcare

Trust-centered service design for non-emergency pediatric decision support

UX Researcher & Designer (Team Project)

Contributed to UX research and service design for a parent-facing healthcare concept: reducing decision-making stress, improving trust in medical guidance, and increasing accessibility for caregivers in non-emergency childcare situations.

Problem

Parents face high cognitive load and uncertainty in non-emergency pediatric decisions. Fragmented online information and limited access to credible guidance undermine trust and caregiver confidence.

My Role & Responsibilities

As a UX Researcher & Designer on a cross-functional team, I contributed to:

  • Qualitative research on parent decision-making, trust drivers, and stress points
  • Interview design, synthesis, and insight extraction
  • Journey mapping and service flows to surface trust and clarity breakdowns
  • Information architecture and content structure for service touchpoints
  • Low- and mid-fidelity prototypes in Figma and Canva

Research & Methods

  • Semi-structured interviews with parents and caregivers
  • Empathy mapping and journey mapping
  • Thematic synthesis (trust, credibility, accessibility)
  • Scenario-based evaluation of non-emergency pediatric decisions

Key insight: Parents reported higher confidence in guidance from medical professionals than from online sources. This drove the team toward human-centered, trust-centered design.

Design Concepts (Team Prototypes)

The team explored service concepts informed by research:

  • 24/7 Parent Hotline — Phone and text access to nurses and pediatric professionals
  • Mobile Pediatric Clinic — Pop-up clinics for underserved urban and rural areas
  • Companion Mobile App — Appointment context, location, and medical history intake

Concepts prioritized accessibility, clarity, and trust over feature complexity.
