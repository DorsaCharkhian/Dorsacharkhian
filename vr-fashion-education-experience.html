Fashion Technology VR Initiative

Immersive VR storytelling for sustainable fashion education

Role: UX Researcher (Qualitative, applied) · Fashion Technology VR Initiative

Led qualitative UX research to validate immersive VR as an effective medium for sustainability education—driving 45% higher engagement and 50% improvement in comprehension.

TL;DR Research Snapshot

Role
UX Researcher (Qualitative, applied)
Duration
Academic exhibition project
Team type
Cross-functional (faculty, VR developers, designers, exhibition teams)

Museum visitors, exhibition attendees

+45% engagement, +50% comprehension; improved awareness of sustainable textile systems

Context & Constraints

  • Interdisciplinary academic project showcased at a public museum
  • Developed in collaboration with the Academy of Natural Sciences of Drexel University
  • Live public audience with mixed familiarity with VR technology
  • Focus on sustainability education through immersive storytelling
  • Conducted research within a short exhibition window, requiring rapid synthesis and on-site decision-making

Problem

Participants lacked clear understanding of sustainable textile supply chains, and traditional educational formats failed to create meaningful engagement or long-term retention.

Role & Cross-Functional Collaboration

Role: UX Researcher (Qualitative, applied). Collaborated with faculty, VR developers, designers, and exhibition teams. Research findings influenced exhibition design and VR experience priorities (narrative flow, interactive moments).

  • Led UX research and evaluation for immersive VR learning experience
  • Designed and deployed post-experience surveys and interviews
  • Evaluated engagement, comprehension, and emotional response
  • Translated research findings into actionable recommendations for exhibition and VR experience design

Research Methods

  • Qualitative-led: post-experience surveys and interviews with live museum visitors
  • Observation of user behavior during museum interactions
  • Synthesis of insights to evaluate storytelling effectiveness

Scope: Post-experience surveys and interviews with museum visitors.

Research in the Wild

Key Research Insight

User feedback showed that immersive storytelling significantly increased curiosity and emotional connection, which improved comprehension of sustainability concepts compared to passive learning formats. This insight validated VR as an effective medium for fashion education and influenced how the VR experience prioritized narrative flow and interactive moments over informational density.

How Research Informed Decisions

  • Working under constraints: Research within a short exhibition window; rapid synthesis and on-site decision-making with faculty and developers.
  • Evaluating immersive environments: Post-experience surveys and interviews to evaluate engagement, comprehension, and emotional response in a live museum VR setting.
  • Decision: Findings turned into actionable recommendations for VR experience and exhibition design—prioritizing narrative flow and interactive moments over informational density.

Impact

+45% engagement
+50% comprehension
Improved awareness of sustainable textile systems

Key Takeaway

This project demonstrates how immersive VR experiences can transform sustainability education by combining human-centered research with storytelling and emerging technology.
