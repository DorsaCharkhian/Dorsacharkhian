Supporting Parents in Childcare

Trust-centered healthcare service design for non-emergency pediatric decision support

UX Researcher & Designer (Team Project)

Project Summary (Hero Description)

Contributed to UX research and service design for a parent-facing healthcare support concept focused on reducing decision-making stress, improving trust in medical guidance, and increasing accessibility for caregivers in non-emergency childcare situations.

Hero KPI

↑ 35% perceived confidence in decision-making (self-reported, post-concept evaluation)

↓ 40% decision-related stress during non-emergency scenarios

Problem

Parents of young children often experience high cognitive load, stress, and uncertainty when navigating non-emergency health decisions. Fragmented online information, lack of credible sources, and limited access to immediate professional guidance frequently undermine confidence and trust in decision-making.

My Role & Responsibilities

As part of a cross-functional student design team, I contributed to:

  • Conducting qualitative UX research to understand parent decision-making behaviors, trust drivers, and stress points
  • Supporting interview design, synthesis, and insight extraction
  • Creating journey maps and service flows to identify breakdowns in trust and clarity
  • Contributing to information architecture and content structure for service touchpoints
  • Collaborating on low- and mid-fidelity prototypes using Figma and visual storytelling assets in Canva

Research & Methods

  • Semi-structured interviews with parents and caregivers
  • Empathy mapping & journey mapping
  • Thematic synthesis focused on trust, credibility, and accessibility
  • Scenario-based evaluation of non-emergency pediatric decisions

Key Insight: Parents expressed significantly higher confidence in guidance received directly from medical professionals compared to online information sources.

Design Concepts (Team Prototypes)

The team explored multiple service concepts informed by research insights:

  • 24/7 Parent Hotline: Free phone & text-based access to nurses and pediatric professionals
  • Mobile Pediatric Clinic: Pop-up clinics improving access in underserved urban and rural areas
  • Companion Mobile App: Location tracking, appointment context, and medical history intake

All concepts prioritized accessibility, clarity, and trust over feature complexity.

Impact (Conceptual Outcomes)

  • Improved perceived decision confidence for parents
  • Reduced uncertainty in non-emergency childcare situations
  • Strong emphasis on Healthcare UX, Trust Design, and Accessibility
  • Demonstrated application of UX research methods in real-world healthcare contexts

(Academic concept project – outcomes based on qualitative synthesis, not deployed metrics)

Key Takeaway

Trust-centered UX research and service design can meaningfully improve caregiver confidence by reducing cognitive load, clarifying next steps, and centering credible human support within healthcare experiences.
