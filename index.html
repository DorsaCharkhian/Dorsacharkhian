About Me

Passionate about the intersection of AI and human experience

I am a UX Researcher and Visiting Assistant Professor at Pratt Institute's School of Information, where I teach Extended Reality: UX & AR/VR and guide graduate students in designing immersive, inclusive, and culturally sensitive experiences.

I hold a Master's in Digital Media with a focus on User Experience and AI from Drexel University, where I led an AI-powered chatbot project that improved accessibility and engagement for international students. My research and design practice combine AI-driven design strategies, usability testing, and qualitative research to craft interactive systems that enhance both usability and empathy.

In my recent work, I've explored how immersive technologies like VR and AR can transform learning experiences—designing VR environments for fashion education and extended reality installations for museums. My goal is to bridge creativity, ethics, and emerging technology to design systems that not only function intelligently but also respond to human diversity with care and inclusivity.
